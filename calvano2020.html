Artificial Intelligence, Algorithmic Pricing, and Collusion

Emilio Calvano, Giacomo Calzolari, Vincenzo Denicolo, Sergio Pastorello
American Economic Review, Vol. 110, No. 10, October 2020  ·  doi:10.1257/aer.20190623

AI pricing algorithms — trained through reinforcement learning alone, with no collusion instructions and no communication — consistently learn to charge supra-competitive prices. The collusion is sustained by autonomous punishment strategies: algorithms retaliate against price cuts, then gradually forgive, in patterns that resemble economic theory far more than chance.

AI 定价算法——仅通过强化学习训练，无需串谋指令，也无需相互通信——始终如一地学会了索取高于竞争性水平的价格。这种合谋由算法自发演化出的惩罚策略所维持：算法会对降价行为实施报复，随后逐步恢复合作，其规律与经济理论的预测高度吻合，绝非偶然。

01

The Central Question

核心问题

Firms increasingly delegate pricing decisions to software. Early repricing tools were rule-based; modern AI systems use reinforcement learning — they develop pricing strategies from scratch, through trial-and-error, with no prior knowledge and no explicit instructions. Amazon deploys such algorithms at scale; so do airline and ride-sharing platforms.

企业越来越多地将定价决策委托给软件。早期的重新定价工具基于规则；现代 AI 系统采用强化学习——从零开始、通过试错自主形成定价策略，无需任何先验知识和明确指令。亚马逊已大规模部署此类算法，航空公司和网约车平台亦然。

This raises a question that matters deeply for competition policy: can AI algorithms spontaneously learn to collude, even when they cannot communicate with each other and have never been told to coordinate? If yes, the traditional antitrust focus on explicit agreements could leave a significant gap.

这引发了一个对竞争政策至关重要的问题：AI 算法能否在互不通信、从未被告知协调的情况下，自发学会合谋？如果是，反垄断政策传统上对明确协议的关注将面临重大漏洞。

The Core Tension

Tacit collusion among humans is considered extremely difficult to sustain — which is why it is mostly legal. The paper's results suggest it may be much easier for algorithms. Current antitrust frameworks were not designed for this world.

人类之间的默契合谋被认为极难持续——这也是它通常合法的原因。本文结果表明，对算法而言这可能容易得多。现行反垄断框架并非为这一世界而设计。

Prior simulation work (Waltman and Kaymak 2008) had observed supra-competitive prices but stopped short of verifying why. The authors argue this distinction is crucial: collusion is not merely high prices — it requires a reward–punishment scheme that gives firms an ongoing incentive to stay cooperative. An algorithm that simply fails to optimize competitively would be replaced quickly by its operator. An algorithm that intentionally sustains high prices through strategic self-restraint is a genuine antitrust concern.

此前的模拟研究（Waltman and Kaymak 2008）观察到了高于竞争水平的价格，但未深究原因。作者认为这一区分至关重要：合谋不仅仅是高价格，还需要一套奖惩机制，使各方持续有动力维持合作。一个仅仅无法找到竞争均衡的算法会被运营者迅速替换；而通过战略性自我克制刻意维持高价的算法，才是真正的反垄断隐患。

The approach is experimental simulation. Because the stochastic dynamic systems generated by reinforcement learning are too complex for closed-form analysis, the authors construct Q-learning algorithms, place them in computer-simulated markets, and study what strategies emerge — running 1,000 independent sessions per parameter configuration.

本文采用实验模拟方法。由于强化学习产生的随机动态系统过于复杂，无法进行解析分析，作者构建 Q 学习算法，将其置于计算机模拟市场中，研究涌现出的策略——每种参数配置运行 1,000 次独立模拟。

02

How Q-Learning Works

Q 学习的工作原理

Q-learning is a model-free algorithm for solving Markov decision problems. An agent observes a state st, takes an action at, receives a reward πt, and transitions to state st+1. The goal is to maximize the discounted sum of future rewards:

Q 学习是求解马尔可夫决策问题的无模型算法。智能体观察状态 \(s_t\)，采取行动 \(a_t\)，获得奖励 \(\pi_t\)，并转移至下一状态 \(s_{t+1}\)。目标是最大化未来折现奖励之和：

\[\mathbb{E}\!\left[\sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\delta^{t}\pi_{t}\right]\]

The key object is the Q-function — a matrix mapping every (state, action) pair to its expected discounted value. For finite state and action spaces, the Bellman equation gives:

核心对象是 Q 函数——将每个（状态，行动）对映射至其期望折现值的矩阵。对于有限状态和行动空间，贝尔曼方程给出：

\[Q(s,a)=\mathbb{E}(\pi\mid s,a)+\delta\cdot\mathbb{E}\!\left[\max_{a'}Q(s',a')\;\middle|\;s,a\right]\]
Q-value = immediate reward + discounted best future value

The learning update rule is a running average. After observing reward πt and next state s', only the visited cell is updated:

学习更新规则是一种滑动平均。在观察到奖励 \(\pi_t\) 和下一状态 \(s'\) 后，仅更新被访问的单元格：

\[Q(s,a)\;\leftarrow\;(1-\alpha)\,Q(s,a)+\alpha\!\left[\pi_{t}+\delta\max_{a'}Q(s',a')\right]\]
α ∈ (0,1) is the learning rate. All other cells are unchanged.

Exploration vs. exploitation is handled with epsilon-greedy: with probability εt the algorithm picks a random action; otherwise it picks the current best. The exploration rate decays exponentially:

探索与利用的权衡通过 ε-贪婪策略处理：以概率 \(\varepsilon_t\) 随机选择行动，否则选择当前最优行动。探索率指数衰减：

\[\varepsilon_{t}=e^{-\beta t}\]
β controls how quickly exploration fades. Early on: random. Later: increasingly greedy.

In a game, multiple Q-learners interact simultaneously. Each treats the others' actions as part of its environment (independent learning). The joint system is non-stationary — rivals are also learning — so convergence guarantees from single-agent theory do not apply. Whether the system settles into equilibrium play must be verified empirically.

在博弈中，多个 Q 学习者同时交互，每个学习者将其他人的行动视为环境的一部分（独立学习）。该联合系统是非平稳的——竞争对手也在同步学习——因此单智能体理论的收敛保证不适用。系统是否收敛到均衡策略，必须通过实证验证。

Initialization

初始化

Q-matrices are initialized to the discounted payoff each action would yield if rivals randomized uniformly — a neutral, uninformative prior:

Q 矩阵初始化为：假设竞争对手随机选择行动时，每个行动对应的折现收益期望值——一个中性、无信息的先验：

\[Q_{i,0}(s,a_{i})=\frac{\displaystyle\sum_{a_{-i}}\pi_{i}(a_{i},a_{-i})}{(1-\delta)\,|A|^{n-1}}\]

The initial state s0 is drawn randomly each session, ensuring results do not depend on a particular starting point.

初始状态 \(s_0\) 在每次模拟中随机抽取，确保结果不依赖于特定起始点。

03

Experiment Design

实验设计

Economic Environment

经济环境

The market is an infinitely repeated Bertrand oligopoly with logit demand. Firms simultaneously set prices each period; demand is given by:

市场是一个无限重复的伯特兰寡头博弈，采用多项式（logit）需求。各企业每期同时设定价格，需求由以下公式给出：

\[q_{i,t}=\frac{\exp\!\left(\dfrac{a_{i}-p_{i,t}}{\mu}\right)}{\displaystyle\sum_{j}\exp\!\left(\dfrac{a_{j}-p_{j,t}}{\mu}\right)+\exp\!\left(\dfrac{a_{0}}{\mu}\right)}\]
a = product quality; μ = horizontal differentiation; a₀ = outside option quality

Each firm's per-period profit is πi = (pi − ci) · qi. State variables are the prices set in the k preceding periods. Action spaces are grids of m equally spaced prices spanning from below Bertrand–Nash to above monopoly.

每家企业的当期利润为 \(\pi_i=(p_i-c_i)\cdot q_i\)。状态变量为前 \(k\) 期设定的价格。行动空间是 \(m\) 个等间距价格点的网格，覆盖范围从低于伯特兰—纳什均衡价格到高于垄断价格。

The Q-matrix for each agent has |S| × |A| = 15² × 15 = 3,375 cells in the duopoly baseline. The parameter grid sweeps 100 × 100 = 10,000 combinations of α and β, each run for 1,000 independent sessions (10 million sessions total). Convergence criterion: the greedy policy (argmax over each state) must be unchanged for 100,000 consecutive periods.

04

Main Results: Algorithms Learn to Collude

主要结果：算法学会了合谋

Measuring Collusion Intensity

衡量合谋强度

The paper introduces a normalized profit gain measure:

论文引入了一个归一化的"利润增益"指标：

\[\Delta=\frac{\pi-\pi^{N}}{\pi^{M}-\pi^{N}}\]
πN = Bertrand-Nash (competitive) profit; πM = monopoly profit. Δ=0 is competitive; Δ=1 is perfectly collusive.
Main Finding

Over the entire 100×100 parameter grid, the profit gain ranges from 70% to 90%. Algorithms consistently learn to charge supra-competitive prices — far above Bertrand–Nash — without any communication, collusion instruction, or prior knowledge. At the representative calibration (α = 0.15, β = 4×10⁻⁶), the average profit gain is Δ = 84.9%.

在整个 100×100 参数网格上，利润增益介于 70% 至 90% 之间。算法在没有任何通信、合谋指令或先验知识的情况下，始终如一地学会了索取高于竞争水平的价格。在代表性参数校准下（α=0.15，β=4×10⁻⁶），平均利润增益为 Δ=84.9%。

Is This Really Collusion, or Just Poor Optimization?

这究竟是合谋，还是次优化？

High prices alone could reflect sub-optimal play — algorithms that simply fail to find the competitive equilibrium. The authors rule this out by computing the "true" best response to each rival's limit strategy and measuring the Q-loss: the percentage of foregone payoff when an algorithm doesn't exactly optimize.

高价格单独来看可能只是反映了次优决策——算法未能找到竞争均衡。作者通过计算针对竞争对手限制策略的"真实"最优应对来排除这一可能，并测量"Q 损失"：算法未完全最优化时损失的收益比例。

The Q-loss on path is typically below 0.5% and never exceeds 1.2% across the entire parameter grid. High prices are not an accident of sub-optimal play — the algorithms are deliberately and near-optimally maintaining collusive prices. Critically, the correlation between profit gain and equilibrium play frequency is only 0.12, confirming that collusion and optimization quality are independent phenomena.

Q 损失在路径上通常低于 0.5%，在整个参数网格上从未超过 1.2%。高价格并非次优决策的意外产物——算法是在刻意且近乎最优地维持合谋价格。关键地，利润增益与均衡策略频率的相关性仅为 0.12，证实合谋与优化质量是两个相互独立的现象。

Key Insight

Algorithms that merely fail to optimize would be replaced by their operators. The algorithms here could compete more aggressively — they just choose not to, because their learned strategies make cooperation the individually rational outcome.

仅仅无法最优化的算法会被运营者替换。本文中的算法完全有能力更积极地竞争——它们只是选择不这样做，因为其学到的策略使合作成为个体理性的最优选择。

Early Training — Random Exploration

Phase 1  ·  Early Training

Exploration: Algorithms Know Nothing

At the start, the exploration rate ε ≈ 1. Both algorithms select prices almost at random, sweeping the entire price grid to discover which actions generate reward. Prices oscillate chaotically near the competitive (Nash) benchmark — there is no coordination, no strategy, only noise.

初始阶段，探索率 ε≈1。两个算法近乎随机地选择价格，遍历整个价格网格以探索哪些行动能带来奖励。价格在竞争（纳什）基准附近剧烈波动——没有协调，没有策略，只有噪声。

Phase 2  ·  Mid Training

The Price War: Undercutting Backfires

Algorithms learn that cutting prices temporarily boosts market share — so both do it simultaneously. The result is a price war: prices crash toward the competitive floor, profits for both collapse. This painful lesson becomes encoded in the Q-matrix as a state to avoid.

算法开始学到降价可以暂时提升市场份额，于是双方同时降价。结果陷入"价格战"：价格双双跌向竞争底线，利润同步崩溃。这一惨痛教训被编码进 Q 矩阵，成为需要回避的状态。

Phase 3  ·  Punishment Learning

Punishment: Retaliation Shapes Strategy

Watch the critical moment: Algorithm 1 deviates, cutting its price to steal market share. Algorithm 2 immediately retaliates — a sharp price cut that makes the deviation unprofitable. After 5–7 periods of punishment, both forgive and prices recover higher than before. This learned punishment–forgiveness cycle is the engine of collusion.

观察关键时刻：算法 1 背叛降价，抢夺市场份额。算法 2 立即报复——大幅降价使背叛无利可图。经过 5–7 期惩罚后，双方"和解"，价格恢复至比背叛前更高的水平。这一惩罚—宽恕循环是合谋的引擎。

Phase 4  ·  Convergence

Tacit Collusion: High Prices, No Communication

The algorithms converge. Both prices settle near the monopoly benchmark, achieving a profit gain Δ ≈ 85%. No messages were exchanged. No instructions were given. The collusion is self-enforcing: each algorithm knows that deviation triggers punishment, so cooperation is individually rational — and invisible to regulators.

算法收敛。双方价格稳定在垄断基准附近，利润增益 Δ≈85%。全程无任何通信，无任何指令。合谋是自我执行的：每个算法都知道背叛会触发惩罚，因此合作是个体理性的最优选择——且对监管者不可见。

05

Anatomy of Collusion: Punishment Mechanisms

合谋解剖：惩罚机制

Verifying Incentive Compatibility

验证激励相容性

The smoking gun for genuine collusion is the presence of punishment strategies. Starting from the long-run prices, the authors exogenously force one algorithm to deviate to its static best response in a single period. Both algorithms then revert to their learned strategies, and prices are tracked for subsequent periods.

真正合谋的"吸烟枪"是惩罚策略的存在。从长期均衡价格出发，作者在单一时期内外生地迫使一个算法背叛至其静态最优应对。两个算法随后恢复各自的学习策略，并追踪后续各期的价格走势。

Punishment Pattern

The non-deviating algorithm cuts its price (punishment). Prices then gradually return to pre-deviation levels over approximately 5–7 periods. In more than 95% of sessions, the punishment makes the deviation unprofitable. The punishment–forgiveness cycle is the hallmark of self-sustaining tacit collusion.

未背叛的算法降价（实施惩罚）。随后价格在约 5–7 期内逐步恢复至背叛前水平。在超过 95% 的模拟中，惩罚使背叛行为无利可图。这种"惩罚—宽恕"循环是自我维持的默契合谋的标志性特征。

Finite Punishment, Not Grim Trigger

有限惩罚，而非冷酷触发

The punishment strategies are notably not grim trigger. Grim trigger — punish forever after any deviation — would be impractical with ongoing exploration, since any random exploration draw would permanently trap firms in the punishment phase. Instead, the algorithms independently learn strategies resembling Abreu (1984) stick-and-carrot: punishment is intense but finite, followed by gradual re-escalation to cooperative prices.

惩罚策略明显不是"冷酷触发"（grim trigger）。冷酷触发——任何背叛后永久惩罚——在持续探索下不切实际，因为任何随机探索都会将双方永久锁入惩罚阶段。相反，算法独立学会了类似 Abreu（1984）"大棒加胡萝卜"的策略：惩罚强烈但有限，随后逐步重新升至合作价格。

Strikingly, both algorithms cut prices after a deviation — even the non-deviating algorithm prices below its pre-deviation level for several periods. For small deviations, prices initially overshoot downward before recovering. This "overshooting" cannot be explained by mechanical mean-reversion; it is structurally consistent with a deliberate punishment design.

值得注意的是，背叛发生后两个算法都会降价——即使未背叛的算法也会在数期内将价格压低至背叛前水平以下。对于小幅背叛，价格最初会超调向下，随后恢复。这种"超调"无法用机械均值回归解释，其结构与刻意设计的惩罚方案高度吻合。

Punishment Ubiquity and Quantification

惩罚的普遍性与量化

The intensity of punishment (average % price cut by the non-deviating algorithm) correlates with the profit gain at 76.2% across the entire parameter grid — the strongest predictor of collusion. Markets where algorithms collude more are precisely the markets where punishments are harder.

惩罚强度（未背叛算法的平均降价幅度）与整个参数网格上利润增益的相关性高达 76.2%——是预测合谋强度最强的指标。合谋程度更高的市场，恰恰是惩罚力度更大的市场。

Strategy Graphs

策略图

With one-period memory, each firm's strategy is a function from 225 possible (price₁, price₂) pairs to one of 15 current prices. The authors represent limit strategies as directed graphs where nodes are states and edges point to successor states.

在单期记忆下，每家企业的策略是从 225 个可能的（价格₁, 价格₂）状态对到 15 个当期价格之一的映射。作者将限制策略表示为有向图，节点为状态，边指向后继状态。

In a representative session, the long-run price pair is an absorbing node: every other state eventually leads back to it. The average path length from any state to long-run prices is 6 steps; the maximum is 18. In 92% of sessions, every possible state converges back to the collusive prices — the self-correcting mechanism is near-universal.

在代表性模拟中，长期价格对是一个"吸收节点"：其他所有状态最终均回归至此。从任意状态到长期价格的平均路径长度为 6 步，最大为 18 步。在 92% 的模拟中，所有可能状态均收敛回合谋价格——自我修正机制近乎普遍存在。

Baseline Validation

基准验证

Two sanity checks confirm the mechanism is genuine. First, with zero memory (k = 0), algorithms cannot condition on past play, so punishment is impossible — simulations confirm near-competitive prices. Second, with zero discount factor (δ = 0), agents are myopic and cannot value future punishment; the profit gain falls smoothly from 85% toward zero as δ decreases, consistent with the theoretical collusion threshold of approximately δ = 0.40 for grim-trigger strategies in this environment.

两项稳健性检验证实了机制的真实性。第一，零记忆（k=0）下算法无法根据过去策略做出条件反应，惩罚不可能发生——模拟结果证实价格接近竞争水平。第二，零折现因子（δ=0）下智能体是短视的，无法重视未来惩罚；利润增益随 δ 降低从 85% 平稳趋向零，与冷酷触发策略约 δ=0.40 的理论合谋临界值一致。

Robustness Across Market Structures

跨市场结构的稳健性

Collusion weakens as the number of firms grows — as theory predicts — but remains substantial. Algorithms continue to collude significantly even with four firms, a stark difference from human experimental results, where collusion "is frequently observed with 2 sellers, rarely with 3, and almost never with 4 or more." If β is reduced to compensate for the larger state space (i.e., allowing proportionally more learning), the triopoly profit gain rises to ~75%.

随着企业数量增加，合谋有所减弱——与理论预测一致——但仍然显著。即使在四家企业的市场中，算法仍持续实现显著的超竞争合谋，与人类实验形成鲜明对比：在人类实验中，"合谋在两卖方时常见，三卖方时罕见，四卖方及以上时几乎从不发生"。若降低 β 以补偿更大的状态空间，三家企业市场的利润增益可升至约 75%。

Asymmetric Firms

非对称企业

Varying firm 2's cost from 0.25 to 1.0 (firm 1 fixed at c = 1), asymmetry reduces Δ modestly — from 85% to about 71% at maximum asymmetry. The gain from collusion is split disproportionately in favor of the less efficient firm: the algorithms coordinate on a solution that does not maximize total joint profit (no side payments), yet each benefits individually. This is not coordination failure — it is an equilibrium of the coordination game without a central planner.

将企业 2 的成本从 0.25 变化至 1.0（企业 1 固定 c=1），非对称性使 Δ 小幅下降——从 85% 降至最大非对称时的约 71%。合谋收益不成比例地向效率较低的企业倾斜：算法协调的解并非最大化总联合利润（无侧支付），但各方均独立获益。这并非协调失败——而是没有中央计划者的协调博弈均衡。

Time Scale and Policy Implications

时间尺度与政策含义

How Long Does Learning Take?

学习需要多长时间？

Convergence typically requires 400,000 to several million periods depending on exploration intensity. Even before full convergence, algorithms begin raising prices above the competitive level well before the learning process completes — collusion emerges progressively, not only at the endpoint of training.

收敛通常需要 400,000 到数百万个周期，具体取决于探索强度。即使在完全收敛之前，算法在学习过程尚未结束时就已开始将价格推高至竞争水平以上——合谋是逐步涌现的，并不仅仅发生在训练终点。

This timescale is a recognized limitation. If one "period" lasts minutes, millions of interactions take years — far longer than typical product cycles. However, the authors note two important moderating factors. First, offline training allows firms to run simulations in compressed time before deployment. When converged algorithms are re-matched with new partners and exploration is shut off, collusion collapses to ~20% initially but recovers in less than one-tenth of the original training time — suggesting that prior training transfers.

这一时间尺度是公认的局限。若一个"周期"持续数分钟，数百万次交互需要数年——远超典型产品周期。然而作者指出两个缓解因素。第一，离线训练允许企业在部署前以压缩时间运行模拟。当已收敛的算法与新伙伴重新匹配且探索被关闭后，合谋最初崩溃至约 20%，但恢复时间不足原始训练时间的十分之一——表明训练知识可以迁移。

Second, in financial markets where periods can last milliseconds, millions of interactions complete in hours. Even a small supra-competitive spread, sustained across millions of automated trades, can generate economically significant extra profits.

第二，在交易周期可达毫秒级的金融市场中，数百万次交互可在数小时内完成。即使是微小的超竞争价差，若在数百万次自动交易中持续存在，也能产生经济上显著的额外利润。

Third, Q-learning is deliberately chosen as the simplest case. More efficient algorithms — deep Q-networks, value-function approximation — update many cells simultaneously and would learn orders of magnitude faster. The paper's results are thus a conservative lower bound on the speed of algorithmic collusion.

第三，Q 学习被刻意选为最简单的情形。更高效的算法——深度 Q 网络、价值函数近似——能同时更新多个单元格，学习速度快几个数量级。因此本文结果是算法合谋速度的保守下界。

Competition Policy

竞争政策

Policy Implications

"Our findings should probably ring an alarm bell for antitrust policy." Current law targets explicit agreements; tacit coordination among humans is largely legal because it is viewed as very hard to achieve. Algorithms may change this calculus fundamentally.

"我们的发现可能应该为反垄断政策敲响警钟。"现行法律针对明确协议；人类之间的默契协调之所以大体合法，是因为被认为极难实现。算法可能从根本上改变这一逻辑。

The authors identify both a risk and an opportunity for regulators. The risk: a world with ubiquitous AI pricing may see more tacit collusion than expected — more false negatives in enforcement. The opportunity: unlike human decision-makers, pricing algorithms can be subpoenaed, tested in simulation, and their strategies formally analyzed. Courts and competition authorities could verify whether a pricing algorithm incorporates punishment logic — something that is inherently impossible with human behavior. This could reduce false positives, potentially shifting the balance of enforcement errors.

作者为监管者识别出了风险与机遇。风险：在 AI 定价无处不在的世界里，默契合谋可能比预期更普遍——执法中出现更多漏网之鱼。机遇：与人类决策者不同，定价算法可以被传唤取证、在模拟中测试，其策略可被正式分析。法院和竞争当局可验证定价算法是否包含惩罚逻辑——这对人类行为而言在本质上不可能。这可能减少误判，有望改变执法误差的平衡。

Key open questions for future research: the effect of richer heterogeneous environments (persistent cost and demand shocks that create signal-extraction problems), the interaction of algorithms from different design families, and the use of faster learning architectures that make the time-scale concern tractable.

未来研究的关键开放问题：更丰富的异质性环境（产生信号提取问题的持续性成本和需求冲击）的影响、来自不同设计族算法之间的交互，以及使时间尺度问题可处理的更快学习架构的应用。

Extension: Market Structure & Algorithmic Collusion

延伸研究：市场结构与算法合谋

The paper's robustness section reports brief results on the number-of-firms dimension but holds the exploration parameter β constant — understating collusion for n = 3 and n = 4 because the larger state space receives proportionally less learning. The following extension replicates the full Q-learning framework of Calvano et al. with the paper's exact parameters, then varies the number of competing firms across 50 independent sessions per configuration, to examine how market structure shapes the intensity and distribution of algorithmic collusion.

论文的稳健性部分简要报告了企业数量维度的结果，但保持探索参数 β 不变——因更大状态空间获得按比例更少的学习而低估了 n=3 和 n=4 时的合谋程度。以下延伸研究以论文精确参数完全复现 Q 学习框架，并在每种配置下运行 50 次独立模拟，将竞争企业数量从 2 变化至 4，以考察市场结构如何影响算法合谋的强度与分布。

Methodology

We replicate the Q-learning duopoly model of Calvano et al. (2020, AER) and vary the number of firms (n = 2, 3, 4), running 50 independent sessions per configuration (α = 0.15, β = 4×10⁻⁶, δ = 0.95, μ = 0.25, m = 15 price points). Profit gain Δ = (πeq − πN) / (πM − πN) measures collusion intensity relative to the competitive (Δ = 0) and full-monopoly (Δ = 1) benchmarks. Equilibrium prices are identified by finding the fixed point of the greedy policy.

Discussion

讨论

The results confirm and extend Calvano et al.'s brief robustness findings. With two firms, the Q-learning algorithm achieves a profit gain tightly consistent with the paper's reported Δ ≈ 85%, validating the replication. The distribution across sessions is wide (IQR spanning roughly 0.75–0.96), reflecting the sensitivity of learned strategies to initial conditions and exploration paths.

结果证实并延伸了 Calvano et al. 简要的稳健性发现。在两家企业条件下，Q 学习算法的利润增益与论文报告的 Δ≈85% 高度一致，验证了复现的准确性。各模拟之间的分布较宽（四分位距约 0.75–0.96），反映了学习策略对初始条件和探索路径的敏感性。

Expanding to three firms produces a meaningful drop in average collusion, as a larger state space (15³ = 3,375 per-firm states vs. 15² = 225 in the duopoly) means each cell receives fewer updates under the same β. The distribution widens substantially, with some sessions achieving near-monopoly profits and others closer to competitive outcomes — suggesting high sensitivity to initial exploration paths in the higher-dimensional learning problem.

扩展至三家企业导致平均合谋水平出现有意义的下降，因为更大的状态空间（每家企业 15³=3,375 个状态，对比双头垄断的 15²=225 个）意味着在相同 β 下每个单元格获得的更新次数更少。分布显著扩宽，部分模拟接近垄断利润，另一些则接近竞争结果——表明在更高维度的学习问题中高度依赖初始探索路径。

The four-firm case shows further decline but remains well above the competitive benchmark in most sessions. This contrasts sharply with human experimental results, where tacit collusion "is almost never observed with four or more sellers." The key difference: algorithms do not suffer from the cognitive and strategic complexity that derails human coordination in large groups. Punishment strategies remain learnable even with n = 4.

四家企业的情形显示合谋进一步下降，但在大多数模拟中仍远高于竞争基准。这与人类实验结果形成鲜明对比，后者发现"在四家及以上卖家时几乎从不观察到默契合谋"。关键差异在于：算法不会因认知复杂性和策略博弈困难而在大群体中协调失败。即使 n=4，惩罚策略依然可以被学习到。

Antitrust Implication

Algorithmic collusion does not require a duopoly. Even in four-firm markets — well beyond the threshold where human tacit collusion breaks down — Q-learning algorithms maintain significant supra-competitive pricing. Antitrust analysis cannot treat large-n markets as safe from algorithmic coordination risk.

算法合谋并不需要双头垄断。即使在四家企业的市场中——远超人类默契合谋崩溃的临界值——Q 学习算法仍能维持显著的超竞争定价。反垄断分析不能将大 n 市场视为免疫于算法协调风险的安全地带。
